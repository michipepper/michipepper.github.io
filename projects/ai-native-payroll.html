An AI-native product for payroll

Designing payroll software where AI lives inside the workflow, from research to a validated product.

Role Product Designer and Builder
Industry AI-Native B2B SaaS - HR Payroll
Year 2026 - Now
A conversational workspace that helps professionals manage the entire payroll cycle, freeing them from repetitive tasks thanks to reliable data.

A mature payroll calculation engine — used by thousands of payroll consultants and specialists in Italy — had inherited a front-end of hundreds of forms and parameter tables, unchanged for a decade. The goal was to design an AI-native experience on top of the existing engine.

The product assists from pre-payroll data collection through to post-payroll compliance: payslip processing, anomaly detection, reporting, employee communications.

The paradigm shift is from doing-per-screen to asking-in-language: the AI orchestrates, the consultant decides, the engine calculates. Each at its own level.

The homepage is
a single request.

No dashboard, no sidebar menus multiplying. A free-form prompt with intent chips for recurring payroll actions, and a block of AI Suggestions that anticipates the state of the portfolio — upcoming deadlines, record changes, payslips ready to validate. Search and the library stay within reach, but they aren't the main path.

The homepage's structure came out of a single observation: in interviews, the first move every morning was always "what do I need to do today?". The old system forced people to infer it from tabs, counters, manual checks in Excel files. The new one is declarative — the AI reads the state and proposes.

  • A single entry pointNo classic dashboard. Benefit: focus. Cost: an expert user initially loses their visual reference points. Mitigated with onboarding and by keeping recent items persistent.
  • Intent chips vs. free-form promptBoth coexist. The expert types, the junior clicks. Neither feels like they're using a "reduced" mode.
  • Proactive suggestionsThe AI surfaces possible actions without executing them. The consultant remains the decision-maker — no action ever runs on its own.

How they interact with the conversational experience

The interaction always starts from a free-form prompt with typed @-mentions (company, employee, period, labor contract). The AI responds with traceable reasoning and opens a side canvas for artifacts worth manipulating — payslips, F24 tax filings, payment batches, reports. Every action is reversible up until an explicit save gate. The traditional UI remains accessible in a parallel tab, for edge cases and for the audits required by regulation.

Every attachment becomes
a structured record.

Clients send data however they can — a Chamber of Commerce extract as a PDF, an Excel sheet, a photo of a handwritten note. Instead of asking them to change their habits, the workspace accepts any format and proposes the structured record. Nothing is saved without confirmation. The same pattern applies to company records, employee records, and monthly data collection.

The three-column pattern is borrowed from other products in regulated industries — source document on the left, reasoning and summary in the center, editable structured record on the right. Every extracted value points explicitly back to the part of the document it came from.

  • Explicit save gateEven when the AI is confident, saving still requires a click. Cost: one extra step. Benefit: no silent writes to the company registry.
  • Missing fields flaggedThe INPS contribution code isn't in the registry extract. The system marks it "to be entered" instead of guessing. No hallucination tolerated on regulated data.
  • Inline editabilityEvery field is editable before saving. The AI proposes, the consultant decides.

Every compliance step
on one screen.

Post-payroll — salary transfers, F24 tax filings, social security bodies, UniEmens — has historically been the most fragmented moment of the cycle, with four separate forms to navigate in sequence. The workspace condenses it into a single checklist screen showing the status of every step, so the operator can see where the cycle stands at a glance and proceed without changing context.

One of the most appreciated processes in the whole product. Pure gold. The strength is that it all runs on a single screen, without switching from one form to another — fast, fluid. Payroll consultant · 200+ employee firm

Post-payroll emerged in discovery as the moment with the biggest perceived value gap. Recomposing compliance steps into a single view turned out to be the most-voted intervention across both personas. The step sequence is customizable to reflect each firm's habits.

  • Customizable orderThe sequence of compliance steps isn't fixed — every firm reorders it. F24 can come before or after social security transfers, depending on habits.
  • Cascading updatesA change to a payslip automatically updates F24 and UniEmens. The consultant is notified, not surprised. A silent recalculation would have been faster but less auditable.
  • Selective deferralSome steps (social security transfers) can be left for later without blocking the cycle. The workflow tolerates interruptions.

On-demand reports
for advisory work.

A payroll consultant doesn't live on the payroll cycle alone — they also live on the value they can show the client. The workspace generates quarterly reports, labor cost analysis, tax incentive simulations, regulatory update notices. These are documents ready to send to the client, with the firm's branding and the consultant's signature. The template is customizable per company.

It's a tool considered very useful — people would like variations like reports with custom KPIs per company, look-ups of regulations and tax incentives, and automated sending. Discovery interviews · 2 payroll consultants

In the current workflow, generating a quarterly report requires manual exports, formatting in Excel, rewriting in Word. The time between the client's request and the finished document is measured in hours. The workspace compresses it to a single prompt, while keeping editorial control over the result.

  • The firm's templateBranding, sections, and disclaimers come from the firm's saved template. It's not a generic AI report — it's the firm's report, generated by AI.
  • Debatable prose, verifiable dataThe commentary narrative is generated; every figure is clickable back to its source (payslip, F24, transaction). Editorial + auditable.
  • Preview before sendingThe report doesn't go out automatically. Preview in the canvas, explicit send. The gate is preserved.

The payslip,
rethought to be read.

A problem that surfaced repeatedly in interviews: consultants and payroll specialists spend hours every month explaining payslip line items to employees. The traditional payslip is designed to be calculated, not to be read — dense columns of codes, abbreviations, amounts without context. Smart Payslip reformats the same information into an understandable summary, with an explanatory narrative generated automatically for each case. It reduces the support requests the firm's clients send in.

The design rules
that held the work together.

Principles applied before every design sprint for AI-native products.

01

Designing for intent

Intent → outcome → refinement. The flow starts from the goal, not from the screen.

02

AI direct

Chat / command interface as the primary entry point.

03

AI assisted UI

Suggestions and prompts embedded within the product's UI.

04

Operational UI

Traditional UI available as a fallback for edge cases and audits.
