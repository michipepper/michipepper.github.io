Redesigning an LMS for a construction giant

An end-to-end redesign of CNH-Case’s Web Academy — the corporate training platform used by its global dealer network.

Role Product Manager & Designer
Industry Enterprise LMS
Scope Discovery → design → hand-off
Year 2023 — 2024

A project with Politecnico di Torino × ISTUD Business School, in collaboration with CNH-Case. Full case study and high-fidelity visuals available on request.

Overview

CNH-Case’s Web Academy is the corporate training platform for their global dealer network. The redesign covered the entire product lifecycle — processes, training methods, and user experience — with the goal of meeting end-user needs more effectively and making learning more intuitive and engaging. The work ran end-to-end: from discovery and research with dealers, through information architecture and interaction design, to high-fidelity UI and hand-off ahead of development.

The problem, framed

The existing platform got in the way of the people it was meant to serve. Discovery surfaced three recurring issues — complex navigation, poor user experience, and low training engagement. Dealers’ technicians and sales reps struggled to find content, tell course types apart, and trust a notification system that was effectively broken.

The platform was a training tool that made training harder.

The solution

A complete overhaul of the platform’s UX and UI, focused on intuitive design and engaging content delivery — giving CNH-Case dealers a more effective and enjoyable learning experience. The engagement spanned two intervention areas, Service Design and Product Design, from October 2023 to January 2024.

The process

The redesign followed a structured arc, each phase feeding the next.

01Research & Analysis — context, service & stakeholder analysis, benchmark, as-is product analysis, field research, qualitative interviews, digital ethnography.
02Ideation & Definition — value proposition canvas, personas, empathy map, user journey, content inventory, information architecture, user flow, co-creation workshop, accessible design.
03Product Design — use cases, sketching, high-fi wireframes, mockups, design system, prototyping.

Outcome & impact

From use cases and sketches to high-fi wireframes, a design system, and an interactive prototype, the redesign translated research into a platform that’s easier to navigate, clearer about each course, and built to keep learners engaged. Validation against the target metrics:
